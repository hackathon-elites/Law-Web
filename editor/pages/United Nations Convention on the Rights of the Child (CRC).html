The United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC), adopted in 1989, is a landmark international treaty that sets forth the fundamental rights and protections to which every child is entitled. The CRC recognizes children as individuals with distinct rights and places their well-being at the forefront of global priorities.




It emphasizes the right to life, survival, and development; the right to be free from discrimination, abuse, and exploitation; and the right to participate in decisions that affect their lives. The Convention promotes the importance of providing children with quality education, adequate healthcare, and a safe environment in which they can thrive. It has been widely ratified by countries around the world, serving as a comprehensive framework to guide legislation, policies, and practices aimed at ensuring that children are nurtured, protected, and empowered to reach their full potential.
